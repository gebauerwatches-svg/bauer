The basics

What does Bauer actually install?

Bauer drops a curated config file (named CLAUDE.md — it tells the AI how to behave for your role) and 4 role-specific skills (small instruction files for common tasks) into your computer’s AI settings. Three of the skills cover the 80% of tasks you’ll do in your role, plus a build-agent skill that walks you through designing custom agents.

How is this different from setting up AI yourself?

By default, when you install the AI tool yourself, it comes with no config and no skills — you’re starting with a blank assistant that doesn’t know who you are or how you work. Bauer drops in the config you’d otherwise spend hours figuring out, and gives you a guided agent builder so you can extend it without learning the underlying file format.

What AI does Bauer use?

By default, Anthropic’s Claude (via the terminal version called Claude Code, running under the hood). Bauer also has a separate open-source install path that uses a local AI model (via Ollama) instead — same Bauer experience, but free and runs entirely on your computer.

How is this different from ChatGPT?

ChatGPT is a website you talk to in a browser. Bauer sets up an AI in your computer’s terminal that can read your files, run commands, and act as an agent — not just chat. It’s also configured for your specific role (small business owner, freelancer, etc.), not a generic assistant.

How big is the download?

The Claude path is small — about 50 MB total. The open-source path is bigger because it includes a local AI model (~4 GB). The Bauer-specific files (configs, scripts) are tiny — under 200 KB.

Cost & safety

How much does it cost?

Bauer itself is free and open source. To use the AI, you have two options:

• Claude Pro subscription ($20/mo) — recommended; predictable, easiest, most capable.
• Open-source local model (free) — more setup, slower, but no monthly cost.

The free claude.ai tier alone won’t be enough for serious use.

Is my data safe?

The AI only reads files you explicitly ask it to. Bauer doesn’t add any data collection — the configs live entirely on your computer. With the open-source local path, nothing leaves your machine. With the Claude path, prompts go to Anthropic’s servers (they don’t train on your data).

Can I really run AI on my own computer? Won’t it be slow?

Yes — the open-source path runs everything locally via Ollama. Smaller open-source models (like Llama or Qwen 7B) work fine on a modern Mac or Windows laptop with 8–16 GB RAM.

Honest tradeoff: local models are slower and less capable than Claude on complex multi-step tasks. Simple tasks (drafting an email, answering a question) finish in seconds. Hard agent tasks might take minutes. For real privacy or zero-cost work, it’s a good fit. For maximum capability, Claude is better.

Will my IT department be okay with this?

Probably yes — Claude is the same AI that many big companies (banks, law firms, consultancies) already use. But if you’re on a managed work laptop and unsure, ask before you install. The open-source path may be easier to approve since nothing leaves your machine.

Install & uninstall

I already have a CLAUDE.md. Will Bauer overwrite it?

No. We back up your existing CLAUDE.md to CLAUDE.md.before-bauer.[timestamp] before writing the new one. Same for any skill folders that collide. Nothing is lost.

How do I uninstall?

Delete ~/.claude/CLAUDE.md and the relevant ~/.claude/skills/ subfolders (or restore your backups). The underlying AI tool stays installed; you can always run it with no config and it works fine.
