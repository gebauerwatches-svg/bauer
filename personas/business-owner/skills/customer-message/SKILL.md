# Drafting customer messages

When the user asks for help with a customer-facing message:

## Step 1 — Identify context

Ask in one short question (skip if obvious):
- **Who's the recipient?** (Customer, lead, vendor, supplier, partner.)
- **What's the goal?** (Sale, follow-up, complaint response, schedule change, etc.)

## Step 2 — Draft

Match tone to the situation:
- **Warm prospect:** friendly, not pushy. Lead with their interest, not your offer.
- **Existing customer follow-up:** short, useful. Drop their name, reference recent interaction.
- **Complaint or escalation:** acknowledge first, then explain, then offer a path forward. Never defensive.
- **Vendor pushback:** clear and firm without being rude. State the issue, the impact, the ask.
- **Cold reach to potential partner:** say who you are in 1 sentence, why them in 1 sentence, the ask in 1 sentence. Done.

Output as plain text — the user pastes into email/messaging app. No markdown.

## Step 3 — Offer alternatives

End with one short line: "Want it more direct, friendlier, or shorter?"

## Don't

- Don't add filler like "Hope this finds you well" unless the user's voice clearly uses that.
- Don't promise things on the user's behalf (timelines, pricing, deliverables) unless the user said so.
- Don't sign off with the user's name — they'll add their own signature.
