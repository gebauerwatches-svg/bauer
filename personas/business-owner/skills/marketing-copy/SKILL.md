# Marketing copy

When the user asks for marketing copy:

## Step 1 — Get the basics

Ask in one question (skip what's clear):
- **Where will this run?** (Twitter/X, Instagram caption, Facebook ad, email, landing page, etc.)
- **Who's it for?** (Existing customers, cold audience, warm leads, etc.)
- **What's the goal?** (Click, reply, sign up, share, awareness.)

## Step 2 — Draft

Match the channel:
- **Twitter/X post:** 1-3 sentences, no hashtags unless requested, end with something a real human would say.
- **Instagram caption:** longer is okay (people scroll captions), 3-5 short lines, hashtags at the bottom or first comment.
- **Facebook/LinkedIn ad:** lead with the hook, end with the action.
- **Email subject line:** 5-9 words, specific over clever. Show 3 alternatives.
- **Email body:** short paragraphs, scannable, one clear ask.
- **Landing page hero:** headline + 1-line subhead. Specific over poetic.
- **Blog post:** intro that earns the read, scannable subheads, conclusion that gives the reader the next step.

## Rules

- **Specific beats clever.** "Cut your invoicing time in half" > "Streamline your workflow."
- **One main idea per piece of copy.** Don't try to say 4 things at once.
- **No buzzwords** (synergy, leverage, ecosystem, robust, scalable) unless the user's brand already uses them.
- **No emojis** unless the user's voice clearly uses them.

## Show alternatives

For short-form copy (social, subjects, headlines): always show **3 versions** in different tones — direct, playful, urgent — so the user can pick.

## End with

"Want me to adjust the tone, shorten, or write a follow-up post?"
