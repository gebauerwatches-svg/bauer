# Launch and announcement copy

When the user is launching something:

## Step 1 — Get the basics

Ask in one question (skip what's clear):
- **What are you launching?** (One sentence.)
- **Who's hearing about this?** (Existing audience, cold audience, both.)
- **What's the channel?** (Twitter, mailing list, Product Hunt, website, all of them.)
- **What's the action?** (Click, sign up, buy, share, just feedback.)

## Step 2 — Output for the channel

For each channel, generate the right shape:

- **Launch tweet (single):** Hook → what it is → why it matters → link. ≤ 280 chars.
- **Launch thread (5-8 tweets):** Hook → the problem → what you built → how it works → who it's for → link.
- **Mailing list:** Subject line (3 options). Body: ~200 words. Personal note → what's new → why → link → "hit reply."
- **Product Hunt tagline:** ~10 words. Specific outcome, not feature.
- **Product Hunt description:** 2-3 paragraphs. Problem → solution → who it's for.
- **Website hero:** Headline (5-9 words) + subhead (1 sentence) + CTA.

## Rules

- **Don't lead with "We're excited to announce..."** Earn the read first.
- **Lead with the user's outcome,** not the feature.
- **Keep it short.** Launches are about getting people to click; the link does the rest.
- **Show 2-3 versions** for any channel where the user has flexibility (especially mailing list subjects, Product Hunt taglines).

## Common mistakes to avoid

- Don't say "today I'm launching" — say WHAT you're launching, then WHO it helps.
- Don't list features. List outcomes.
- Don't apologize ("I know it's not perfect, but..."). Be confident.

## End with

"Want it more bold, more humble, or pitched at a different audience?"
